I regret not contributing to open source early in my career. On my journey to the path of employment, I had a difficult time putting my foot in the door or any door at all to be exact. One thing I've noticed is I have little to no connections with anyone and that reason alone lowered my chances of being employed. While stuck in this job searching phase I feel like I was stuck between a rock and hard place but then I realized maybe I should contribute to opensource and expose myself to the community.

## Finding a community

I was excited to contribute but the question was to what org? There were alot to choose but one org caught my eye, it was based on the filipino community working hard to preserve transparancy on the filipino government plus they use typescript. 

## Finding things to fix

Read their code of conduct and contribution guide, it was pretty easy for beginners like me, what was hard is actually finding an issue and making a pr.

So I started with actually running the necessary dependencies to run the repo locally, the way I did it was halfed-ass but nonetheless it worked so I started searching for issues. I noticed that there were parts of the website that didn't idicate whether data was loading or not so the fallback elements were pretty much a nothing burger.

I started making a draft and while I was making it my doubts started to arise, "what if I get laughed at on making this", "you're gonna make a fool of yourself" - these phrases started to fill my head and this is when I know that If I'm not gonna make this issue then I'll be stuck in rut forever so I went ahead and sent the issue with anxiety on my side. I waited.... and waited annndddd about 2 days later I got notif.

I was about my issue being tagged as a bug and it being assigned by me to fix it! I was really happy I got the opportunity to fix it, yippie! 6 Hours later I drafted a solution and made my pr. It got noticed but I'm still wating for a reviewer but still. I'm happy that I can just do that. My confidence towards myself really went up.
